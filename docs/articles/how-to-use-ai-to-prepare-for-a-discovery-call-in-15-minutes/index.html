How to Use AI to Prepare for a Discovery Call in 15 Minutes

By Greg Grand · July 14, 2026

The Short AnswerAI helps prepare for discovery calls by quickly aggregating and summarizing public company data, recent news, and industry trends. It identifies potential challenges and suggests tailored questions. This enables sales professionals to enter conversations informed, build credibility rapidly, and focus on deep problem identification rather than basic research, ultimately improving call effectiveness and closing rates.

Key Takeaways

  • Effective discovery preparation requires specific intel, not generic research.
  • AI tools can automate data gathering, freeing reps to focus on strategy.
  • A structured, AI-assisted process helps identify specific company challenges and tailor your questions.
  • AI is a powerful assistant, but human interpretation and genuine curiosity remain essential for connection.
  • Turn AI insights into a concrete call agenda and personalized talking points to drive more meaningful conversations.

Why does pre-call preparation still matter?

Sales isn't a guessing game. Every discovery call is a chance to prove you belong in the room, a chance to earn trust. Most reps walk into these conversations under-researched, relying on charm or a standard script. That approach is a pipeline killer.

You wouldn't trust a doctor who prescribed meds before asking what hurts. So why do sales reps still pitch before understanding the problem? It costs you deals.

Preparation shows respect. It tells the prospect, without you saying a word, that their business matters. It lets you ask questions that pierce through the surface, questions that uncover the real, painful truth of their situation.

How does AI help with discovery call preparation?

The old way of preparing for a discovery call meant hours of digging. You'd scroll through LinkedIn profiles, sift through corporate websites, maybe read a few news articles. It was manual, it was time-consuming, and often, you still missed the truly important insights.

AI changes that. It becomes your research assistant, doing the grunt work in minutes. It doesn't replace your brain; it augments it.

AI doesn't do your thinking. It just clears the brush.
It gives you the raw materials, the specific data points, so you can focus on building a strategy, not just collecting facts.

AI's role is simple: it quickly gathers and synthesizes vast amounts of public information. It identifies patterns, keywords, and potential areas of concern that you might have overlooked. This means you walk into the call knowing more, asking smarter questions, and sounding like a genuine expert.

What exact steps do I take to use AI for discovery prep?

This process provides a precise, repeatable framework for extracting critical intelligence. It puts you in control of your own narrative. Here is a five-step process:

  • Step 1: The Intel Fetch. Input the target company's name and primary contact into your AI tool. Instruct the AI to scrape public data for recent news, earnings calls, strategic announcements, and any major personnel changes, especially at the executive level.
  • Step 2: The Pain Point Profile. Ask the AI to synthesize this information. Direct it to identify 2-3 common challenges or stated strategic objectives that relate to your solution. For example, if you sell marketing automation, ask for insights into their current marketing spend, recent campaigns, or customer acquisition goals.
  • Step 3: The Competitor Scan. Prompt the AI to identify their primary competitors. Then, ask for insights into recent product launches, funding rounds, or significant news from those competitors. This provides context on market pressures facing your prospect.
  • Step 4: The Question Builder. Based on the identified pain points and competitor insights, instruct the AI to generate a list of 5-7 open-ended discovery questions. These must not be generic. They should be specific to the company's apparent context. You want questions that make them think, not just recite.
  • Step 5: The Objection Anticipator. Finally, ask the AI to suggest common objections or concerns a company with this profile might have regarding a solution like yours. This helps you prepare your responses and frame your value proposition more effectively.

What information should AI find for me before a call?

The goal is to move past surface-level facts. You want AI to dig for specifics that create a talking point, a genuine curiosity. Here are the categories of information you should expect your AI assistant to provide:

  • Recent Company News: Mergers, acquisitions, new product launches, financial results, press releases. Look for growth initiatives or, conversely, signs of struggle.
  • Industry Trends: How is their specific sector performing? Are there regulatory changes, technological shifts, or economic pressures impacting their business?
  • Competitor Activity: Who are their main rivals, and what are those rivals doing that might pressure your prospect? New funding, market share shifts, or product innovations are relevant.
  • Executive Insights: Recent interviews, LinkedIn posts, or public statements from key leaders. What are their stated priorities? What challenges keep them up at night?
  • Job Postings: A company's open roles often reveal their strategic investments and skill gaps. This can signal where they are focusing resources and where they need help.

Each piece of information is a thread. Your job is to pull the right threads, not just collect them.

How do I build a strong discovery call agenda with AI's help?

A weak agenda makes for a weak call. A clear agenda gives you control and shows your prospect you value their time. AI can help you refine this structure, making it highly personalized. Think of it as a blueprint for a productive conversation.

First, define the core purpose of your call based on the AI-generated insights. Is it to validate a specific pain point? To understand a strategic shift? Then, create a proposed structure that moves logically through the conversation. A solid agenda follows a simple progression:

  • Permission-Based Opening: State your understanding of their business context (informed by AI), propose the agenda, and ask if it works for them. Example: "Based on your recent announcement about X, I thought we could discuss Y. Does that sound right?"
  • Problem Definition: This is where you deploy your AI-generated questions. Focus on understanding the depth and impact of their challenges. Ask about current processes, existing solutions, and the costs of inaction.
  • Impact Discussion: Translate the problem into business terms. What is the financial cost of their challenge? What is the impact on their team, their customers, their growth?
  • Solution Alignment (Brief): Only after a thorough diagnosis, you can briefly touch on how your solution might address their specific, validated problems. This is not a pitch; it is a hypothesis.
  • Next Steps: Always end with a clear, mutually agreed-upon next action. This could be a follow-up meeting, a demo, or a data-gathering exercise.

Your agenda should not be a secret. It should be a collaborative roadmap. AI helps you draft a precise, relevant map for them to follow.

How do I turn AI insights into human connection?

This is where the human element becomes critical. AI provides data. You provide the empathy, the interpretation, the nuanced questioning. Think of AI as your high-powered telescope. It shows you the stars, but you still need to understand what you are seeing, and you need to share that wonder with someone else.

The data from AI gives you an informed starting point. It allows you to say, "I noticed your company recently announced X. How has that impacted Y for your team?" That specific question, born from AI research, builds immediate credibility. It tells the prospect you did your homework.

But then you listen. Really listen. The AI can give you questions, but it cannot hear the hesitation in their voice, or pick up on the unstated priorities. Your role is to build on the AI's foundation, using your human skills to dig deeper, to empathize, to connect. This is about precision, not automation. This is about making you a sharper salesperson, not a lazier one.

Frequently Asked Questions

Can AI replace human sales skills in discovery?

No, AI cannot replace human sales skills. AI automates data gathering and suggests relevant questions, making human reps more informed and efficient. However, human intuition, empathy, active listening, and the ability to build rapport remain essential for effective discovery. AI acts as a powerful assistant, not a substitute for genuine human connection.

What are the risks of using AI for call preparation?

The main risks include relying too heavily on AI without human verification, receiving outdated or inaccurate information, and creating overly generic questions. Sales professionals must cross-reference AI data, add their own insights, and use AI-generated questions as a starting point, not a rigid script. Always apply critical thinking to AI outputs.

How quickly can AI prepare me for a discovery call?

Using a focused process, AI can prepare you for a discovery call in under 15 minutes. It quickly aggregates company news, industry trends, and competitor insights, then helps generate tailored questions. This speed allows sales teams to prepare thoroughly for many calls without sacrificing time, making preparation a standard, not a luxury.

Does AI help with follow-up after a discovery call?

Yes, AI can assist with follow-up. It can summarize call transcripts, identify key action items, and suggest relevant resources or next steps based on the discussion. AI can also help draft personalized follow-up emails that reference specific points from the conversation, ensuring your post-call communication remains relevant and impactful.
