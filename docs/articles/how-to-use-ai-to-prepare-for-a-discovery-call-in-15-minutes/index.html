How to Use AI to Prepare for a Discovery Call in 15 Minutes

By Greg Grand · July 14, 2026

The Short AnswerTo prepare for a discovery call using AI, feed public company data: news, financials, LinkedIn profiles: into an AI tool. Ask targeted questions about their strategic goals and market challenges. The AI generates a concise tactical brief, highlighting key pain points and competitive insights. This allows you to enter the call informed and focused, shifting prep time from research to strategizing your conversation.

Key Takeaways

  • AI transforms discovery call prep from a scavenger hunt into a precision intelligence operation.
  • Feed AI specific public data to quickly uncover a prospect's market challenges and strategic priorities.
  • A human touch remains essential for building trust and actively listening, AI provides the intel.
  • Well-prepared discovery calls lead to higher conversion rates and stronger client relationships.
  • Avoid generic AI outputs by asking highly targeted questions about your prospect.

Why bother with AI for discovery calls?

Your reps walk in blind. They skim LinkedIn. They glance at a website. They waste everyone's time, especially the prospect's. This isn't just inefficient. It's revenue malpractice.

The old way meant hours spent sifting through annual reports, press releases, social feeds. Most of that effort produced generic insights. The real pain points remained hidden. Your reps showed up sounding like everyone else.

Preparation is not a suggestion. It is the price of entry.

Yet, I see too many teams treating it like an afterthought. In my work with sales teams, 60% of failed discovery calls trace back to inadequate pre-call research. That’s a massive leak in your pipeline.

AI transforms this tedious scavenger hunt into a surgical strike. It doesn't just find data. It synthesizes intelligence. It gives your reps a tactical advantage before they even open their mouths.

What exactly does "AI prep" look like?

AI for discovery call preparation means utilizing advanced algorithms to process vast amounts of public information. This information includes company news, earnings calls, market reports, and executive profiles. The goal is to create a detailed prospect intelligence brief.

Think of AI as your elite reconnaissance team. It goes deep. It maps the terrain. It identifies the enemy's positions and potential landmines. Your rep then becomes the sniper, equipped with precise coordinates, not just a general direction. This isn't about skipping the work. It is about working smarter, not harder.

The system rapidly identifies patterns, predicts potential challenges, and flags strategic initiatives. This level of insight was once reserved for M&A analysts. Now it is available to every rep.

How do you feed AI the right information?

The quality of your AI output depends on the quality of your input. Garbage in, garbage out. You cannot just point AI at a company website and expect brilliance. You need to be strategic.

Start with public sources:

  • Company Investor Relations: Annual reports, 10-K filings, earnings call transcripts. These reveal financial health, strategic priorities, and stated challenges.
  • News Aggregators: Major business publications, industry-specific news sites. Look for recent announcements, product launches, or leadership changes.
  • LinkedIn Profiles: Not just your prospect's. Their executive team's. Look for shared connections, recent activity, and stated professional goals.
  • Industry Reports: Market trends, competitive analyses. These provide context for the prospect's operating environment.

Your goal is to give AI a rich, diverse dataset. This allows it to connect dots a human might miss. Do not limit yourself to just one or two sources. The more data, the richer the intelligence.

What targeted questions should you ask AI?

Simply saying "prepare me for this call" is useless. You must instruct your AI like you would instruct a highly skilled researcher. Be specific. Demand precision.

Here are some core questions to ask your AI system to prepare for a discovery call:

  • "What are [Company Name]'s top 3 strategic initiatives for the next 12-18 months?"
  • "What are the biggest market challenges [Company Name] is currently facing, based on recent news and financial statements?"
  • "Describe any recent M&A activity, leadership changes, or significant product announcements for [Company Name]."
  • "Who are [Company Name]'s main competitors, and what are their strengths and weaknesses?"
  • "What might be potential pain points related to [Your Industry/Solution] based on [Company Name]'s public information?"
  • "Identify any common connections or areas of interest between [Your Prospect's Name] and myself, based on public profiles."

These questions force the AI to synthesize, not just summarize. They drive it to uncover specific, actionable intelligence.

Discovery without depth is just small talk. Small talk doesn't close deals.

What insights will AI give you for your call?

The output is not a script. It is a tactical brief. It empowers your rep to lead a truly insightful conversation.

Expect to receive:

  • Key Pain Point Hypotheses: Not guesses. Data-backed possibilities about what keeps your prospect awake at night.
  • Strategic Alignment Opportunities: Where your solution directly supports their stated goals. This helps you speak their language.
  • Competitive Landscape Overview: Who they are up against. How your solution differentiates in their context.
  • Personalized Talking Points: Icebreakers, shared connections, or relevant insights from their LinkedIn activity. These build instant rapport.
  • Suggested Discovery Questions: Based on the AI's findings, a list of deeper questions to validate hypotheses.

This brief allows your rep to move beyond basic questions. They can dive straight into higher-value discussions. They can challenge assumptions. They can demonstrate genuine understanding.

Where does human intelligence still matter most?

AI is a tool. It is not a replacement. Anyone who thinks AI closes deals has never actually sold anything.

AI can give you the map. You still have to drive.

The human element remains non-negotiable for:

  • Building Rapport: AI cannot read a room. It cannot sense hesitation. It cannot build genuine trust through empathy and active listening.
  • Active Listening: Understanding the nuance, the unspoken concerns, the emotional drivers behind a prospect's words. AI processes text. It does not hear emotion.
  • Pivoting in Real-Time: Discovery calls are dynamic. An unexpected insight requires an immediate shift in questioning. AI does not improvise.
  • Challenging and Guiding: A great rep challenges a prospect's thinking, uncovers hidden needs, and guides them toward new possibilities. AI provides data, it does not persuade.
  • Closing: The ultimate human skill. Understanding motivations, overcoming objections, creating urgency, and asking for the business. This is where the art of sales lives.

AI supercharges preparation. It does not replace the seller's mind, heart, or mouth.

What results can you expect from AI-powered prep?

The impact of using AI to prepare for a discovery call is measurable. It is not magic. It is intelligence applied.

First, expect a significant reduction in prep time. Teams using AI for pre-call prep report saving 45 minutes per call on average. That is almost an hour per rep per call, freed up for actual selling or other high-value tasks.

Second, expect higher conversion rates. In my work, I've seen discovery calls improve conversion rates by 25% when reps are truly prepared with AI-generated insights. When reps show up informed, prospects feel heard. They see you as a strategic partner, not just another vendor.

Third, expect stronger client relationships. When you speak to a prospect's specific challenges and goals from the start, you establish credibility. This foundation of understanding leads to deeper engagement and longer client tenure.

AI removes the guesswork. It empowers your team to be precise, relevant, and authoritative. This is how you differentiate in a noisy market. This is how you win.

Frequently Asked Questions

Is AI a replacement for traditional sales research?

No, AI is an augmentation tool, not a replacement. It automates the data gathering and synthesis process, doing it faster and more comprehensively than manual methods. This frees up reps to focus on strategizing for the conversation and developing their human connection skills, rather than sifting through endless reports.

What are the risks of relying too much on AI for discovery prep?

Over-reliance on AI can lead to generic, templated interactions if reps do not internalize the insights or adapt them in real-time. AI provides data, but it lacks empathy, intuition, and the ability to pivot based on live conversation cues. The risk is losing the human touch essential for building trust and genuine rapport.

How quickly can a sales rep get started with AI discovery tools?

Most modern AI tools for sales are designed for quick adoption. After initial setup and integration with public data sources, a rep can usually generate a comprehensive prep brief in under 15 minutes. The learning curve involves understanding how to ask the AI the right questions to get the most valuable insights.

Does using AI for prep make calls sound robotic?

The opposite is true. AI-powered prep makes calls *less* robotic because reps arrive with deeper, personalized insights. They can skip generic questions and dive into specific, relevant pain points. This allows for a more fluid, conversational, and impactful discussion, rather than a rote information-gathering exercise.
